// Core Capabilities

Built for compliance, settlement, and transparency.

Each module in CARBON maps to a failure mode in today’s EU ETS stack: fragmented registries, bilateral opacity, and delayed verification.

⛓

On-Chain Credit Registry

Every EUA is tokenized once. Ownership, transfer history, and retirement state live in one immutable registry instead of 27 siloed national systems.

⚡

Direct Company-to-Company Trading

Surplus sellers and deficit buyers settle atomically on-chain. No broker layer, no duplicated records, no reconciliation lag between counterparties.

🔍

Full Market Transparency

Trade flow, pricing, and inventory movement are publicly readable in real time — giving regulators and markets the visibility the compliance cycle currently hides for months.

✅

EU ETS Compliant by Design

Protocol rules mirror allowance issuance, transfer constraints, and retirement obligations so compliance is enforced by the exchange itself.

// Workflow

From emission cap to retired credit in four steps.

The demo illustrates the same lifecycle modeled here: issuance, tokenization, exchange, and permanent retirement at the compliance boundary.

01
CAP

Limit is Set

The EU sets a hard annual cap on GHG emissions. Allowances (EUAs) are issued — one per permitted tonne of CO₂ equivalent.

02
TOKENIZE

Credits Go On-Chain

Each EUA is tokenized on CARBON. The on-chain registry becomes the single source of truth — no parallel paper record.

03
TRADE

Companies Trade Directly

Surplus sellers list credits. Deficit buyers buy them. Settlement is atomic, instant, and public. No broker. No 18-month lag.

04
RETIRE

Credits Are Burned

At the compliance deadline, credits are submitted and burned on-chain permanently. Reuse becomes cryptographically impossible.
