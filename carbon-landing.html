// The Current System

The EU ETS operates as a cap-and-trade system.

A hard limit is set on total yearly greenhouse gas emissions. Allowances are issued — one per tonne of CO₂ equivalent. Companies who cut emissions sell surplus credits. Companies who overpollute must buy more.

// The Problem

But here is the structural flaw.

The compliance cycle creates a built-in transparency gap that no one talks about. Regulators are flying blind — and the architecture was never designed to fix it.

⏱

9–18 Month Reporting Lag

Emission data for a given year doesn't reach regulators for up to 18 months. No one sees what's actually happening in the market in real time.

👁️‍🗨️

Opacity

Intercompany trades happen invisibly during the compliance window. Regulators can't separate real hedging from speculative risk.

⚠️

Verification Delays

Third-party verifiers must physically audit emission data — expensive, slow, introducing months of uncertainty before any credit is confirmed clean.

🔄

Fraud & Double Counting

Without a single shared registry, the same credit can be presented to multiple buyers. Double-counting is structurally possible — and has occurred.

🏛️

No Single Source of Truth

Data lives across 27 national registries, broker systems, and bilateral contracts. A complete market picture is impossible in real time.

📉

Market Risk Mispricing

Because positions are invisible until compliance deadlines, price discovery is impaired. Late-cycle buying creates artificial volatility.

// How It Works

From emission cap to on-chain credit in four steps.

01
CAP

Limit is Set

The EU sets a hard annual cap on GHG emissions. Allowances (EUAs) are issued — one per permitted tonne of CO₂ equivalent.

02
TOKENIZE

Credits Go On-Chain

Each EUA is tokenized on CARBON. The on-chain registry becomes the single source of truth — no parallel paper record.

03
TRADE

Companies Trade Directly

Surplus sellers list credits. Deficit buyers buy them. Settlement is atomic, instant, public. No broker. No 18-month lag.

04
RETIRE

Credits Are Burned

At the compliance deadline, credits are submitted and burned on-chain permanently. Reuse becomes cryptographically impossible.
